# SKILL.md — how to win in an era where the tech is cheap and distribution is everything

> Source: https://x.com/i/article/2020790794534440961

## What this skill does
This skill enables you to build and sustain a competitive product in a market where technical barriers to entry are near-zero. It teaches you to prioritize consistent, targeted distribution over product perfection, ensuring your offering reaches the right users—those who will pay, use, and advocate for it—before competitors replicate your tech or drown you in noise.

## When to use
- You’ve built an MVP using AI tools (Claude Code, Perplexity, etc.) and need to avoid being erased by the next viral tool.
- Your product has technical parity with competitors but isn’t gaining traction.
- You’re launching a SaaS, app, or tool and have < $10k budget for marketing.
- You’re seeing high engagement (likes, views) but zero conversions or paid users.
- You’re considering a “big launch” but lack a repeatable system to sustain attention.

## Core concepts
1. **Tech is table stakes**: Building an MVP now takes hours, not months. The competitive advantage no longer lies in code complexity.
2. **Distribution = consistent exposure to the right audience**: Not views, followers, or virality—only measurable reach to your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).
3. **Distribution is not marketing**: Marketing pushes messages; distribution ensures the right people see your product in context, repeatedly, through trusted channels.
4. **Three proven distribution channels**: UGC (user-generated content), Paid Marketing, Organic Content—each must be optimized for ICP, not volume.
5. **Attention is currency**: The most valuable asset isn’t tech—it’s the ability to make your product unavoidable to your target users through repetition and credibility.
6. **Elon Musk effect**: Valuation and survival are increasingly tied to narrative control and public perception, not revenue or product specs.

## Step-by-step workflow
1. **Define your ICP precisely**: Who exactly will pay for this? (e.g., “Freelancers who invoice $5k+/month and hate QuickBooks” — not “small businesses”).
2. **Build a minimal, functional MVP** using AI tools (Claude Code, Perplexity, etc.) in < 24 hours.
3. **Choose one distribution channel to start**:
   - *UGC*: Find 5–10 micro-creators (1k–10k followers) in your ICP’s niche. Offer free access + $50–$100 to post authentic use cases.
   - *Paid*: Run 3 small Meta/Google ads ($5/day each) targeting ICP interests. Track CPM and conversion rate. Scale only if CPA < LTV.
   - *Organic*: Post 1 piece of daily content (thread, short, tweet) for 30 days. Focus on problem/solution framing, not product features.
4. **Track conversion rate from exposure to paid user**: Use UTM links or unique promo codes. Ignore vanity metrics (likes, shares).
5. **Double down on the channel with highest conversion rate**. Kill the others after 2 weeks of poor results.
6. **Launch a “big moment”** (e.g., video, product update) only after 3+ weeks of consistent organic/UGC activity. Aim for 10k+ views in 24h.
7. **Repeat**: Every 30 days, add one new distribution tactic. Never stop.

## Rules and pitfalls
- ✅ DO: Measure distribution by conversion rate to paying users, not impressions.
- ✅ DO: Use UGC from real users—not influencers with fake engagement.
- ✅ DO: Replicate what works. If one ad creative converts, make 10 variants of it.
- ❌ DON’T: Spend money on brand videos, domains, or “launch events” without prior ICP validation.
- ❌ DON’T: Chase virality. A meme with 1M views that doesn’t convert is a cost center.
- ❌ DON’T: Assume tech superiority = market dominance. Most startups fail because they built something nobody needed, not because they built it poorly.
- ❌ DON’T: Ignore retention. Distribution brings users in; product keeps them. But distribution is the gatekeeper.
